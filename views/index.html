How do natural resources become federal revenues?

The federal government issues leases granting rights to explore for natural resources which may exist on federal lands. In return, part of the sales value for many of these commodities is paid back to the government as “royalties” (along with other types of revenues), for the benefit of the American people.

Where does the money go?

The answer is: it depends. Laws treat revenues from offshore natural resources and onshore natural resources differently. Laws also treat different commodities differently. For example, for oil and gas, Federal and local governments get royalties that are a percentage of sales. For many minerals such as gold, copper and silver, the governments get mining claim fees but no ongoing royalties.
