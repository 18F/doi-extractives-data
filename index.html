So where do these revenues go?

The answer is
it depends. Laws treat revenues from offshore natural resources and onshore natural resources differently. There are set percentages and amounts from each that go certain places every year.

Where do offshore revenues go?

For natural resource revenues from offshore locations, 27% of revenues from within 8(g) boundaries (pdf) goes to the state that the revenues came from, $150 million goes to the Historic Preservation Fund, up to $900 million goes to the Land & Water Conservation Fund, some goes to the Federal agency that manages the area, and the remainder goes to the U.S. Treasury.

Where do onshore revenues go?

For natural resource revenues from onshore Federal land, for most parts of the country 49% goes to the state that the revenues came from, 40% goes to the Reclamation Fund, some goes to the agency that manages the land, and 11% goes to the U.S. Treasury. For revenues from Alaska, 89% goes to the state. For revenues from Indian Country, 100% of revenues are returned to the Tribes or individual Indian land owner.

How do natural resources become
Federal revenue?

If there are natural resources under (or even suspected of being under) Federal lands, the U.S. Department of the Interior grants leases to companies to explore for and extract these products to be sold.

In return, part of the sales value is paid to the Federal government for the benefit of the American people. These are called “royalties.” There are other revenues involved, such as rents, fees, and penalties, but by far most of the money comes from these royalties.
