Revenue collected by the Bureau of Land Management and the Office of Natural Resources Revenue

Claim-Staking Fees

Claim-staking fees cover the government’s administrative costs in the claim-staking process for mining on public domain lands.

Processing Fee $20, Location Fee $37, Initial Maintenance Fee $155.

When mining on acquired lands, miners must pay a one-time $6,500 Prospecting Permit Fee.

Annual Fees

For locatable hardrock minerals, those mined on public domain lands, miners must pay annual per site maintenance fees to maintain the claim.

Annual Maintenance Fee $155.

Bonus

A bonus is the amount paid by the highest bidder for a natural resource lease. No bonuses are paid for locatable hardrock mining.

Rent

Rent is an annual payment for leasing lands or waters. No rent is paid for locatable hardrock mining.

For hardrock mining on acquired lands, miners pay annual fees and rent.

Prospecting Fee $0.50 per acre. Rent $1.00 per acre.

Royalty

A royalty is a payment for the natural resources extracted, determined by a percentage of the resources’ production value. No royalties are paid for locatable hardrock mining. Mining hardrock minerals on acquired land is exempt from minimum production and minimum royalty requirements under Title 43 in the Code of Federal Regulations.
