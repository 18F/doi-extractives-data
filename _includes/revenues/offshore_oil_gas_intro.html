Offshore Oil & Gas

The Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act (OCSLA) of 1953 grants the Secretary of the Department of the Interior (DOI) the authority to manage offshore energy resources and to develop regulations to carry out that authority. The Secretary has delegated those responsibilities to two bureaus within DOI, the Bureau of Ocean Energy Management (BOEM) and the Bureau of Safety and Environmental Enforcement (BSEE), respectively, and has delegated revenue management functions to the Office of Natural Resources Revenue (ONRR). Together BOEM, BSEE, and ONRR manage, regulate, and collect revenue from 33 million leased acres across the Outer Continental Shelf.
