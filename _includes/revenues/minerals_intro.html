Nonenergy Minerals

The General Mining Act of 1872, legislation that regulates gold, silver, cinnabar, and copper, in addition to “other valuable deposits,” was signed into law by Ulysses S. Grant. Despite some amendments, this legislation governs extraction of many nonenergy minerals, in particular hardrock locatable minerals on federal public domain lands. The Minerals Leasing Act of 1920 limited the scope of the 1872 Act, providing different regulations for energy-producing minerals such as coal, oil, and natural gas. The Acquired Lands Mineral Leasing Act of 1947 and the Federal Land Policy and Management Act (FLPMA) of 1976 govern mining on acquired lands. Hardrock mineral extraction on federal lands is currently regulated by the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) within the US Department of the Interior (DOI).
