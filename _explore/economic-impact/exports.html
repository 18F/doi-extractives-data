Exports

In 2013, the U.S. exported $137,558 million in petroleum end-use goods, 8.6% of all exports totaling $1,592,784 million. The U.S. is now a net exporter of petroleum products and coal, although still a net importer of natural gas and crude oil. Natural resource commodity exports, meaning commodities that underwent minimal processing, made up approximately $22,000 million in goods produced and sold abroad.
